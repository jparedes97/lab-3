About the Park

The New York Aquarium was founded in 1896 in Castle Clinton in Battery Park, at the southern tip of Manhattan. In 1902, the New York Zoological Society (now Wildlife Conservation Society) assumed ownership of the aquarium. Although the location of the Aquarium has moved, first from Castle Clinton to the Bronx Zoo before moving to its current home on Coney Island in 1957, the WCS commitment to connecting New Yorkers to ocean wildlife at the Aquarium has never wavered.

Wild Encounters

Wild Encounters are a way to meet personally with amazing animals, and learn about our extraordinary work right from our keepers. Chat with the keepers and get answers to your most challenging questions Meet some of the stars of our Aquatheater show Get close to amazing animals Support the New York Aquarium while connecting with wildlife like never before No two Wild Encounters are ever the same – sea for yourself!

Things to do

Discover the sea that never sleeps in the waters right across the boardwalk. Or take in one of Coney Island’s best shows at the Aquatheater. The Aquarium is home to sharks, rays, sea otters, octopus, jellyfish and more. Plan a visit to explore for yourself.
